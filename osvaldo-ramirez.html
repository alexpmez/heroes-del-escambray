Osvaldo Ramírez

García

Osvaldo Ramírez was captain of the rebel army in the fight against Batista. Unhappy with the communist turn of the triumphant revolution, the struggle against it began, he was considered the most audacious guerrilla of the Escambray. While other guerrilla leaders had been waiting under orders not to fight, Osvaldo Ramírez had organized his guerrilla squads into combat units, attacking and harassing the enemy at every opportunity. Without waiting for weapons, with nonexistent resources, in a few weeks Ramírez became the best-known guerrilla fighter in the hills of Las Villas.

Osvaldo Ramírez was a fibrous peasant, toasted by the sun of the Sierra. He was born on July 6, 1921 in Guayos, near Sancti Spíritus, province of Las Villas. He worked as a truck driver for the sugar mills and later became a rental driver. In 1958 he rose in the Escambray against Batista. After the triumph of the Revolution, Ramírez was promoted to captain of the National Revolutionary Police, assigned to a position of supervision in the province of Matanzas. In the summer of 1960, he rose again in the Escambray after the combat at the Sitio de Juana.

Shortly after rising, Ramírez was captured by a military unit. When he was transferred for interrogation, this risk taker guerrilla leader launched himself into a ravine, in front of the surprised eyes of his captors. The jump towards a high precipice, was a suicidal action. But the guerrilla of Guayos was lucky. The dense weeds and the branches of the trees stopped their vertiginous fall. Bruised and with his body covered with wounds, Osvaldo Ramírez fled through the brush, while a squad of soldiers, astonished at his suicidal courage, watched him flee from the top of the ravine. That audacity was the seal of Osvaldo Ramírez. From the beginning of the guerrilla struggle. Ramírez was always the warrior of the mountain, born for combat.

In a public appearance, Fidel Castro offered amnesty to Osvaldo Ramírez, saying demagogically: "We want to convince them that they are wrong, and if Osvaldo Ramírez deposes his weapons, we will guarantee his life." From the Sierra del Escambray, the response was typical of the hero guerrilla: "If Castro wants to speak, let him lay down his arms and go up to the Escambray. We will guarantee your life. "Osvaldo Ramírez answered in a clandestine interview.

The guerrilla leader Osvaldo Ramírez survived dozens of encirclements. He became for Castro's army, the most coveted prey. In the month of April, Ramírez was betrayed by Filiberto Cabrera Carranza, known by Pancho el Grande, a diminutive employee of the Meyer hospital. When hundreds of militiamen surrounded the guerrilla camp in Las Aromas de Velázquez, intense fighting ensued. Little by little, to the fire of the machine guns VZ and PPCha, the militia closed the knot. Again, Osvaldo Ramírez, the most wanted guerrilla of the Escambray, vanished in the brush, mocking the fence.

A few hours later, in a nearby glen, a corporal of militias detected a movement in a thicket. The corporal, a freckled blond man nicknamed El Yanqui, fired a shot into the glen. The solitary lead found a body: that of the Chief Maximum of the Escambray guerrillas. On April 16, 1962, Osvaldo Ramírez, the only guerrilla fighter to whom Castro had offered amnesty if he laid down his arms, was dying. Today, his body rests in an unknown grave on Cuban soil.
