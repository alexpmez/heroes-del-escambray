Humble peasants rise against dictatorship
Peasants rise against dictatorship

Disenchanted by the communist turn of the revolution for which many of them had fought, the humblest men of Cuba, the peasants of the Cuban countryside, took up arms in an attempt to recover a betrayed revolution.

They fought with tenacity against communism
Brutal struggle

Peasant war encompassed the six provinces of the Island, being the largest military campaign carried out in Cuba since the beginning of the Republic in 1902. Since the time of the spanish civil wars, it hasn't been such a fiercely fight on Cuban soil.

Misunderstood and ignored by it's own people
Misunderstood and ignored

Without effective means to counter the Castro's propaganda machinery, which branded them as criminals and bandits. These brave Cubans died in combat, by firing squad or served long sentences, misunderstood and ignored by a large part of the same people they were trying to liberate.

In an unequal struggle they were defeated
An Unequal Struggle

Without adequate and poorly armed supplies, harassed by well-armed enemy hosts, the guerrillas were eventually defeated despite the fury and tenacity with which they fought.
